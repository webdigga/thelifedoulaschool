---
title: Protocols of a Life Doula
description: 'The protocols of a Life Doula: the standing promises behind The Life Doula Bridge. We do not push, promote, judge or force; we welcome all faiths and none.'
---

Every teaching needs its guardrails: the promises that decide not what is taught, but how. These are the protocols of a Life Doula. They bind me first, and they are offered to anyone who learns or shares this work.

## We do not push

No one is ever hurried across the bridge. Readiness cannot be manufactured, and a door opened by pressure leads nowhere worth going. The teaching waits, for years if it needs to.

## We do not promote

This path does not advertise itself as better than any other, and it does not recruit. It is laid out in the open for whoever finds it useful; the light is left on, and that is all.

## We do not judge

Wherever you are standing when you arrive, that is an acceptable place to be standing. Faith or none, wreckage or calm, belief or doubt: nothing here measures you, and nothing here requires confession.

## We do not force

No practice is compulsory, no belief is required, and no part of the teaching asks you to override your own knowing. Anything that does not serve you is yours to set down without apology.

## We welcome all faiths and none

The bridge is a middle path. It borrows the best of the spiritual and the practical without demanding membership of either. Every tradition, and no tradition, can cross it without being asked to change flags.

## We serve the crossing, not the crowd

A Life Doula serves the person in front of them: their pace, their questions, their thresholds. The work is measured in single crossings, not in numbers.
