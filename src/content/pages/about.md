## The teacher behind the bridge

I am Rebecca Sarah, though most people call me Becca. For years I worked as a Life Doula, walking beside people through the living moments between birth and death: the crossroads, the losses, the quiet collapses and slow rebuilds that no one hands you a map for. That work, and the road that led me to it, became a philosophy I now give my whole time to teaching: The Life Doula Bridge, the practice of bridging heaven and earth in an ordinary life.

I no longer offer 1:1 services. Nothing on this site is for sale. What I learned is here to be shared: through this site, my videos, and in time a full course built on the 8 Bridging Keys.

## My story

### Losing myself slowly

It was a relatively minor family argument that first made me question whether I wanted to be here any more, but in truth it was the end of a long, gradual process of losing myself that began in childhood. I grew up in a family of six that struggled to connect; I often felt unseen and unsure of my place. When I became a full-time carer for my mother after her stroke in 2004, being needed gave me purpose and something that felt like love, and my world narrowed around it. My creativity and self-love were set aside. When she died suddenly, I was left without direction or reason, and for years afterwards I kept falling into the same pattern: seeking my worth in helping others while never nurturing myself.

### Rock bottom, and a leap of faith

On Christmas Day 2021 I reached my lowest point. Completely alone, I questioned whether life was worth continuing. What surfaced, strangely, were the old cultural stories about angels, and the thought that perhaps I had a guardian angel watching over me. I took a leap of faith and reached out. Accepting that I was loved and supported unconditionally, by a presence I could not see, was the beginning of everything that followed.

### The slow work of healing

With that support I began [shadow work](/healing-and-shadow-work/) and guided healing, facing my codependent patterns and low self-esteem honestly. I reclaimed my own power a little at a time: in my care work, where asking for guidance in hard moments made me steadier and less alone; and in my finances, where I finally faced £35,000 of debt from my caregiving years and, with help from a compassionate debt adviser, cleared the burden and my fear of the future with it.

### From healing to teaching

With the weight lifted, I turned to the question underneath it all: what is my life actually for? The answer arrived through writing and through work: my calling was to help people navigate the living moments between birth and death, with love, guidance, and practical support. Years of doing exactly that, together with everything my own crossing taught me, distilled into The Life Doula Bridge: the [8 Bridging Keys](/8-bridging-keys/), the [protocols](/protocols/), and the [three core beliefs](/core-beliefs/). Teaching them is the work of the rest of my life.

## Experience that shaped the teaching

- More than 20 years assisting people with disabilities to live independently
- 10 years working with children and families
- 5 years in floristry and horticulture, where garden learning began
- A lived road through grief, anxiety, OCD, bulimia, debt, and suicidal ideation, and out the other side

## Words I teach by

> Oneness is having everything taken away yet knowing you still have everything.

> You are not learning anything new, you are just remembering.
