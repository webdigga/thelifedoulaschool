Health is more than just the absence of illness. A comprehensive wellbeing review considers physical, mental, emotional and even social aspects of life. Balancing these dimensions helps you function well and cope better with life's challenges.

We are often too busy to really assess how we are doing physically and emotionally. Taking time to think about your health and wellbeing provides an opportunity to reflect on and improve lifestyle habits such as diet, exercise, sleep and stress management. Regular reviews, whether through self-assessment or professional check-ups, can help identify potential physical and emotional issues before they become serious.

Making small, positive changes in these areas can have a big impact on your overall wellbeing.

Take time to think about:

- Are you getting enough sleep, and are there ways to improve this?
- Is your daily schedule allowing for self-care, rest and time to eat nutritious meals?
- Are stress and worry affecting your health?
- Are you getting regular exercise, or do you need support to do this?
- Have you missed any important appointments or been putting off health check-ups?

## Goal Setting and Motivation

Reviewing your health helps you set realistic goals, whether it is improving fitness, managing weight or reducing stress. Tracking progress towards these goals can be motivating and rewarding. There is more on this in [setting and achieving goals](/setting-and-achieving-goals/).

## Intuitive Self-Care and Taking Inspiration from Nature

Plants and animals know instinctively how to pace themselves to conserve energy for when they need it. Their ability to know when to race ahead and when to rest and keep safe is crucial for survival. All aspects of nature have active growing periods and resting or dormant periods so that they can be at their best when they need to be.

## Listening to the Body, Not the Nagging Voice in Your Head

When you feel good and your energy is high, do as much as you can, so that when you are feeling low you can give yourself permission to rest. It is often the negative voice in our head that tells us we need to keep pushing through.

## Pacing Yourself Through the Year

- Make the most of the long summer days and learn to incorporate more rest in the longer, darker seasons.
- Take exercise such as walking outside as much as possible to help with hormone and mood changes in the winter.
- Are you doing too much 365 days a year? Yes, we live in a 24/7 world, but do you have to take on so much all the time?
- Can you say no when you already have a lot to do?
- It is OK not to be across everything all the time; people who really care about you will not mind.
- Learn the art of pacing: if you burn out, you will come to a complete stop.

## Signs It May Be Time to Seek Help from a Healthcare Professional

- Headaches
- Irritability
- High blood pressure
- High heart rate
- Appetite changes
- Weight changes

## Practical Ways to Carry Out Your Own Review

- Book and keep the appointments you have been putting off.
- Learn the basic health checks that we can all do at home.
- Start a health journal to work towards goals or to monitor how you are doing. A health journal is useful information to take to your next check-up, helping you and others understand what your normal parameters are.
- If you have lost confidence with cooking, start small and rebuild the habit of preparing a nutritious meal.
- Explore ways to exercise safely, or try a new activity gently if you are feeling nervous.
- Explore mindfulness and meditation practices.

A non-medical wellbeing review is never about medication or diagnosis; it is about building a gentle routine of self-assessment and self-care.
