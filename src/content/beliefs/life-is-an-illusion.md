The first core belief holds that life, as our senses report it, is not the whole story. What we call reality is a stage set: vivid, convincing, and deeply felt, yet built for a purpose. The costumes and scenery matter because of what they allow the soul to learn, not because they are the final truth about us.

Seen this way, the belief is not bleak but freeing. A prison cell with beautiful wallpaper is still a prison cell; being willing to see beyond the illusion is more than most people ever consider doing. Naming the illusion loosens its grip. Setbacks become scenes rather than verdicts. The question shifts from "why is this happening to me?" to "what is this teaching the part of me that lasts?"

This belief walks hand in hand with the everyday. It does not ask you to abandon the world or treat it carelessly; the classroom deserves respect precisely because the lessons are real. Heaven and earth stay bridged: one bank gives the experience, the other gives the meaning.
